Clinical Cosmetology courses are educational programs that focus on the medical procedures and therapies aimed at improving the physical appearance of individuals. These courses are typically designed for medical professionals, such as doctors, nurses, & dentists, who want to specialize in aesthetic medicine.

Courses Module

Module 1: Skin Science Basics

This module builds a strong base in cosmetology, focusing on skin anatomy and physiology essential for diagnosing and treating skin conditions. It covers:
Pigmentation & Hyperpigmentation: Causes, contributing factors, and modern treatment protocols.
Aging Skin: Stages, symptoms, and anti-aging solutions including injectables and lasers.
Skin Types & Care: Identification of different skin types and creating personalized skincare plans.
Students will gain clear insights into essential concepts and techniques for clinical success.

Chemical Peels

This module covers types, functions, and application of chemical peels. Students will gain hands-on practice and learn about post-care and managing side effects.

Cosmeceuticals

Students explore the science behind cosmeceuticals, their benefits (anti-aging, brightening, hydration), key ingredients, and how to recommend suitable products.

Hormones & Skin Health

Learn how hormones and vitamin deficiencies affect skin. Students will identify imbalances and explore treatments to improve skin condition using vitamins.

Derma Planning

Students get practical training in derma planning, learning proper tools, techniques, and procedures for safe and effective exfoliation.

Medical Facials

This section covers types of medical facials, benefits, and complete procedure steps—from skin prep to exfoliation, extraction, and post-care.

Module 2: Advanced Aesthetics & Regenerative Therapies

This module advances students’ skills with modern techniques and technologies in clinical cosmetology. Building on Module 1, it introduces:
Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP) Therapy: Science and use of PRP for skin and hair rejuvenation.
Micro-Needling: Hands-on training in techniques, protocols, and safety measures.
Students will deepen their aesthetic expertise through practical exposure to these high-demand treatments.

Meso Therapy

Covers types and uses of mesotherapy for skin, hair, and cellulite. Students gain hands-on experience in safe and effective administration techniques.

Injectable in Cosmetology

Students learn basics of facial contouring using injectables like fillers and neuromodulators, understanding their types, uses, and theory fundamentals.

Lasers Therapy

Covers laser types like Q-Switch, Diode, and IPL for treatments like rejuvenation, tattoo removal, pigmentation, and hair reduction with safety protocols.

Hair Reduction Techniques

Teaches laser wavelengths, protocols, and techniques for safe and effective hair reduction, with practical training on laser device operation and settings.

Module 3: Advanced Applications

This module introduces students to trichology, focusing on hair and scalp health. It begins with an understanding of hair anatomy, types, and growth cycles, essential for diagnosing and treating hair-related concerns. Students learn to identify conditions like hair loss, dandruff, infections, and formulate appropriate treatments. Emphasis is placed on non-surgical and medical hair loss solutions, with training in assessment, treatment planning, and patient education.

Hair Medication

Covers oral, topical, and supplement-based treatments for hair loss. Students learn correct usage, prescribing basics, and monitoring outcomes for effective hair regrowth.

Hair Transplantation

Introduces techniques like FUE & FUT, including donor site evaluation, graft harvesting, and post-procedure care. Ethical aspects and patient criteria are also discussed.

Platelet Rich Plasma (PRP)

Focuses on non-surgical hair restoration methods. Students learn science, protocols, and application techniques for PRP and mesotherapy in treating hair thinning and loss.

Low Light Laser Therapy (LLLT)

Explores photobiomodulation for hair regrowth. Students study LLLT devices, treatment protocols, and safety, gaining insights into this non-invasive approach to hair loss.
